What is The Phoenix Project?

Our mission is to provide effective and reliable open-source solutions to members of the Songbird & Flare Network community, allowing the community to actively shape their respective token economies. We are dedicated to driving positive change and fostering vibrant token ecosystems that promote sustainability and value creation.

Central to our vision, The Phoenix Project comprises of four key elements:

The Phoenix Foundation

A Decentralized Autonomous Organization (DAO) comprised of creators and developers within the Songbird & Flare Network Community who actively collaborate to implement innovative solutions, such as the Songbird Phoenix Token (sPHX).

FeatherSwap DEX

The first deflationary & not-for-profit Decentralized Exchange built on the Songbird Canary Network. Designed to maximise returns for liquidity providers whilst simultaneously contributing to a deflationary token economy.

Proof-of-Burn Mechanism

Utilizing our unique Proof of Burn mechanism, the Phoenix Token can help address the significant challenges faced by an inflationary blockchain network. By intentionally and irreversibly burning native tokens - ecosystem participants help create scarcity, enhance price stability & provide economic incentives for the underlying network.

Community Initiatives

The Phoenix Foundation actively supports research & development as well as community-driven initiatives that contribute to our mission. Through strategic partnerships and collaborations we work towards the widespread adoption and integration of our deflationary models, fostering an environment of collaboration, transparency and shared knowledge.

The Phoenix Token

The Phoenix Token is a deflationary ERC20 token on the Flare & Songbird Networks. It serves as a utility token and an open-source smart contract, enabling network participants to actively contribute to the growth and sustainability the Network.

How to MINT

The Phoenix Token can be minted using our unique Proof-of-Burn smart contract mechanism.

Participants who wish to mint The Phoenix token can do so by burning the native coin of a network via The Phoenix smart contract. The ratio is set at fixed 1:1, meaning that for every coin burned, an equal amount of Phoenix token is minted and issued to the participant.

The Phoenix Foundation

The Phoenix Foundation is a decentralized autonomous organization (DAO) and community-driven initiative that supports and fosters the development and growth of The Phoenix Project within the Flare and the Songbird Networks. It serves as a guiding force behind the project, providing resources, governance, and strategic direction to ensure its success in both networks.

The foundation's primary objective is to advance the principles and goals of The Phoenix Project, which include addressing inflation challenges, promoting price stability, and creating a sustainable token economy within the Flare Network and Songbird Networks. It collaborates with developers, contributors, and stakeholders within the ecosystems of both networks to drive innovation, facilitate community engagement, and provide the necessary infrastructure for the project's implementation.

Join the Community

The Phoenix Project - Founders Edition NFT Series is a collection of 1,000 NFTs that grants unique privileges to the holders. By owning one of these NFTs, individuals gain the ability to become a founding member of The Phoenix Project, and may take part in the matters of governance that pertain to The Phoenix Foundation.

As a Founder within The Phoenix Project Community Discord and as a member of The Phoenix Foundation - Founders have the ability to add their credentials to The Phoenix Foundation section of the website. This role also provides exclusive access to a dedicated Founders channel, where foundation members actively participate in discussions, collaborate and contribute their insights on matters related to The Phoenix Project's directions. The aim of The Phoenix Foundation is to decentralize project governance and to ensure community involvement in decision-making processes.
